The live academic-medical-center HIV-nutrition program — end-to-end

DRAFT • v0.1 • generic-AMC framing; UConn-named upgrade pending Vanessa clearance

The patient-journey timeline depicts the 9-stage patient journey through the live academic-medical-center HIV-nutrition program running on Cena's substrate today. The timeline reads left-to-right by temporal stage (Referral → Re-assessment); each stage crosses four top-to-bottom actor lanes (Patient action / Clinical staff action / Cena substrate action / Provenance artifact created). The fourth bottom lane carries the audit-trail artifact written at each stage and serves the legal/compliance reader's deep-link path from /how-the-substrate-works/provenance-and-accountability.

Lanes

  • Patient (top lane) — the patient as the protagonist of their own care, mediated by the partner's clinical team. Patient cells use role language not identity language.
  • Clinical staff (second lane) — the partner-institution's clinical team at every stage where a clinical call is required. Every cell uses HDG row 4 partner-clinician phrasing; clinical accountability stays partner-owned throughout the program.
  • Cena substrate (third lane) — the substrate's structural action at each stage. Verbs are structural (writes, captures, generates, flags, logs, places, ingests, registers). The substrate-action lane uses the haven canon `.diagram-box--substrate` variant (teal-800 fill, white text) uniformly across all 9 cells to encode "by the system, automatically."
  • Provenance artifact (bottom lane) — the audit-trail artifact written to the audit-trail store at each stage. Cells are nominal (artifact name + attribution + timestamp); a complete scan of this lane reads as the program's defensible trail end-to-end.

Stages

  • 01 Referral — patient enters via the partner's referral pathway; partner intake reviews eligibility against IRB protocol; substrate ingests and normalizes the intake record; referral receipt with source attribution and timestamp written to audit store.
  • 02 Enrollment — patient consents at in-person enrollment visit; partner's program coordinator obtains consent and registers the participant; substrate registers the enrollment and captures the consent record; consent record + enrollment artifact + clinician signature + timestamp written to audit store.
  • 03 Assessment — patient completes intake assessment with partner's RD; partner's RD assesses nutrition history and clinical indicators; substrate captures the assessment record and structures the clinical data; assessment record + clinical-staff attribution + timestamp written to audit store.
  • 04 Care plan — patient receives care plan reviewed with partner's clinician; partner's clinician approves the substrate-generated recommendation and retains accountability; substrate generates the recommendation with peer-reviewed citations attached; recommendation record + cited literature + clinician approval + timestamp written to audit store.
  • 05 Ordering — patient receives order confirmation; partner's care coordinator confirms the order against the approved plan; substrate places the order to the partner's existing fulfillment contract; order record + fulfillment-contract attribution + timestamp written to audit store.
  • 06 Fulfillment — patient receives fulfillment per the partner's delivery channel; partner's navigator monitors fulfillment exception-driven; substrate logs the delivery confirmation; fulfillment confirmation + delivery confirmation + timestamp written to audit store.
  • 07 Adherence check-ins — patient engages adherence check-ins via the patient-facing interface; partner's care coordinator intervenes per substrate-flagged signal; substrate captures the adherence signal and flags when threshold crosses; adherence-signal capture + intervention record (if triggered) + timestamp written to audit store.
  • 08 Outcome capture — patient completes outcome measures per protocol cadence; partner's clinician reviews the outcome record; substrate logs the outcome record with measurement methodology captured; outcome record + measurement methodology + timestamp written to audit store. Outcome VALUES are deferred to /proof; this stage's claim is the artifact's existence.
  • 09 Re-assessment — patient returns for re-assessment per protocol schedule; partner's clinician closes the loop and revises care plan as needed; substrate flags the re-assessment trigger per protocol criteria; re-assessment trigger record + criteria + timestamp written to audit store. A subsequent care-plan revision produces a fresh recommendation record at stage 04 of the next cycle.

Relationships

  • The substrate lane carries the load-bearing temporal axis — 8 inter-stage arrows show flow across the 9 substrate cells.
  • The patient, clinical staff, and provenance lanes do not carry inter-stage arrows; those lanes read as the cells themselves and the temporal axis is shared with the substrate lane.
  • Stage 09 (Re-assessment) produces a soft back-edge to Stage 04 (Care plan) in operational reality — a re-assessment can trigger a care-plan revision. For v0.1 this back-edge is documentation-only (named in the substrate-action cell at Stage 09 and in the Re-assessment fragment) rather than a visible loop arrow.

Generic-AMC framing throughout — every cell uses "the partner" / "the partner's clinician" / "the partner's RD" / etc. The strict-superset UConn-named upgrade is deferred to a later edit when Vanessa's red-line clearance lands per outstanding-partner-input.md item #1. Stage 07 (adherence check-ins) and stage 08 (outcome capture) carry the HDG §8 watchlist cells — the v0.1 copy is written conservatively; substantive claims must hold under technical-evaluator scrutiny against current Spark + Ava shipped fidelity before launch.
