About

What Cena is.

Cena is a three-person company building clinical intelligence infrastructure for food-as-medicine.1 Agents do the throughput work; humans provide accountability and trust.

The thesis

Cena is built on the thesis that agents are primary brainpower.2 Agents-first means agents do the throughput work — assembling clinical intelligence from source records, writing the audit trail for every recommendation, running second-system clinical checks, and producing the reports each partner program's reporting cadence requires. Humans provide what agents structurally do not: accountability and trust. The substrate operates in the open; every recommendation it produces carries the trail that makes the recommendation defensible. The work that would size a conventional company sits inside the substrate, not on a team org-chart. That's why the team is three people.

The team

Three by design — agents-first means the throughput work is agent-led and the team stays small. Roles are functional; titles say what each person does.

Clinical accountability

During pilots, partner-institution clinicians retain clinical accountability for every recommendation Cena's substrate generates.3 Cena's role is the audit-grade trail that makes that accountability defensible — see provenance and accountability for the regulatory substantiation. The substrate writes one entry per clinical action — input data, reasoning step with citation, recommendation, clinician approval, downstream outcome capture, re-assessment trigger; the sample audit trail makes the artifact concrete. The trail is the structural foundation; the partner's clinical authority sits on top of it. This is the agents-first thesis instantiated in the clinical domain: agents do the throughput; humans hold accountability.

Governance

Brand decisions sit with a sole approver, supported by expert agents that handle research, design, execution, and maintenance.4 There is no separate brand-team contact; brand questions route through the warm-intro motion that brings most partner conversations in. Clinical governance follows the accountability model above — partner-institution clinicians retain authority during pilots, with Cena's substrate as the trail underneath. The governance shape this page describes reflects Cena at pilots-planning stage. When the structure changes, the page changes.
