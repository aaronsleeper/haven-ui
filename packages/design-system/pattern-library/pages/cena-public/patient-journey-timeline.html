1. The composition

The four swim-lanes top-to-bottom: Patient (patient action), Clinical staff (partner-institution clinicians; HDG row 4 phrasing), Cena substrate (structural verbs; teal-800 substrate variant), Provenance artifact (the audit-trail artifact written at each stage). Stages left-to-right per IA §7 locked order.

Nine-stage patient journey through the live academic-medical-center HIV-nutrition program, rendered as four horizontal swim-lanes — patient, partner clinical staff, Cena substrate, and provenance artifact — so the legal/compliance reader can scan only the provenance lane and the technical evaluator can scan only the substrate lane without losing the cross-cutting view.

Elements

  • Stage columns (left to right) — Referral, Enrollment, Assessment, Care plan, Ordering, Fulfillment, Adherence check-ins, Outcome capture, Re-assessment.
  • Patient lane (top) — what the patient does at each stage; role language, not identity.
  • Clinical staff lane — partner-institution clinicians; partner clinician / coordinator / RD retain accountability per Cena pilots-stage accountability model.
  • Cena substrate lane — structural work the substrate does at each stage (captures, records, logs, generates, writes, confirms, flags); teal-800 substrate variant.
  • Provenance artifact lane (bottom) — the audit-trail artifact written at each stage; the lane the legal/compliance reader scans to verify a complete defensible trail.

Relationships

  • Within each lane, time flows left-to-right; muted arrows connect consecutive stages.
  • Across lanes at a single stage (column), each actor's action is co-temporal: read top-to-bottom at any stage to see what the patient, partner clinician, substrate, and provenance artifact each do at that moment.
  • No cross-lane edges — cross-lane reading is by column, not by edge.

Reading order — first scan the stage columns left-to-right to grasp the journey shape, then scan any single lane left-to-right to follow one actor through all nine stages. The substrate-lane verbs (captures, logs, generates, writes, confirms, flags) and the provenance-lane noun artifacts together substantiate the page's claim that the substrate produces an audit-grade trail at every stage.

2. Cell-content annotations (writer-facing notes for Phase 3b)

Cell labels above carry the load-bearing claim shape. Phase 3b page composition (the actual /reference-program page) inherits these labels as the timeline's canonical cell content; hover/tooltip detail and below-the-fold annotations expand within the per-cell discipline below.

  • Patient lane. Role language only — "Receives referral," "Enrolls, consents," "Reviews care plan." Never "patient does X by name." Identity language is HDG-disallowed.
  • Clinical staff lane. HDG row 4 phrasing — "Partner clinician routes referral," "Partner RD conducts assessment," "Partner clinician approves plan." Cena never holds clinical accountability in pilots; the lane label always names the partner-institution role.
  • Substrate lane. Structural verbs only — captures, records, logs, generates, writes, confirms, flags. Avoid vendor-flattery verbs (delivers, provides, empowers, orchestrates). Each cell describes what the substrate does, never which vendor underlies it (HDG row 3).
  • Provenance lane. Noun artifact + write moment — "Referral receipt + timestamp," "Consent record + signature," "Recommendation + clinician approval." The lane reads as a defensible trail when scanned standalone.
  • Per-stage hedging. Any cell whose substrate or provenance claim does not match current shipped Spark + Ava fidelity must hedge or remove before launch (per audit + HDG packet §3.1 Audit-log automatic capture + §8 future watchlist). Particularly: adherence check-ins (stage 7) and outcome capture (stage 8) are the easiest to overclaim — verify against current implementation before Phase 3b composition.
