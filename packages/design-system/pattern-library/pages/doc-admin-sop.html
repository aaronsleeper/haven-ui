The admin role's exact responsibilities are not yet finalized. The tasks below are a starting proposal drawn from the pilot's operational needs; treat them as a draft to react to, not a settled scope.

The administrator keeps the program's operational backbone in place — staff readiness, system access, materials, data requests, and subcontractors — so the clinical team can focus on participants.

Scope

What this role covers, and where its boundaries are (proposed).

For Cena's program administrator(s) supporting the UConn Health pilot.
Covers Staff training and credential tracking, system-access provisioning, materials-distribution tracking, UConn data-export requests, and subcontractor onboarding.
Does not cover Clinical work (Registered Dietitian), participant care coordination (Care Coordinator), or participant enrollment intake (Enrollment & Onboarding SOP).

Recurring administration tasks

The standing responsibilities that keep the program compliant and running.

  1. 1

    Track staff training and credentials

    Where: In the staff training tracker — every staff member and MPH student.

    Expected result: Everyone who touches participant data has current CITI / IRB training on file.

    Note: Someone without current training must not be given access to participant data.

  2. 2

    Provision role-based system access

    Where: In the system admin settings.

    Expected result: Each person has exactly the access their role requires under the IRB protocol — no more.

  3. 3

    Track materials distribution

    Where: In the materials-distribution tracker.

    Expected result: A record of which educational materials and recipe booklets (English / Spanish) reached which participant.

  4. 4

    Fulfill UConn data-export requests

    Where: Through the data-export process.

    Expected result: Requested data delivered to UConn in a non-proprietary format within 15 days.

    Note: The 15-day turnaround is a contract requirement — start promptly when a request comes in.

  5. 5

    Onboard subcontractors with signed BAAs

    Where: Through the subcontractor onboarding checklist.

    Expected result: Kitchen, delivery, and clinical subcontractors are onboarded with a signed BAA before they handle any participant data.

When someone needs access

The one branch that comes up often — and the gate that protects participant data.

Their CITI / IRB training is current
Provision the access their role requires under the IRB protocol.
Their training is missing or expired
Hold access until training is complete. No participant-data access without current training.

Terms used in this SOP

Plain-language definitions.

CITI / IRB training The required human-subjects research training everyone must complete before working with participant data.
IRB protocol The study's approved rules for how the research is run, including who may see what data.
BAA Business Associate Agreement — the contract a partner signs before they're allowed to handle participant data.
Role-based access Giving each person only the system access their job needs — nothing more.
Non-proprietary format A common, openable file type (not locked to one vendor's software) that UConn can use without Cena's tools.
