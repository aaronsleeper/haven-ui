## Long paragraph (~120–160 words)

> Medically tailored meals are not a supplement to clinical care — they are clinical care.
> Research consistently shows that food insecurity, poor nutritional quality, and
> diet-disease mismatch account for a significant share of preventable hospitalizations and
> readmissions in the United States. For patients managing chronic conditions such as
> diabetes, heart failure, kidney disease, or cancer, the right meal at the right time can
> be as consequential as the right medication at the right dose. Cena Health was built on
> this premise. Our platform connects health systems, insurers, and community health
> organizations with a network of medically certified kitchens, enabling the delivery of
> individualized, dietitian-designed meals at the speed and scale that modern care management
> requires. Every meal that leaves a Cena kitchen has been reviewed against a patient's
> current care plan, screened for allergens, and verified against nutritional targets before
> it is packed and dispatched.

---

## Multi-paragraph block (~250–300 words)

> Patients in the Cena Health program receive meals that are designed, reviewed, and updated
> by a team of registered dietitians working in close coordination with their primary care
> providers. Each meal plan reflects the patient's active diagnoses, current medications, lab
> values, and self-reported food preferences. Plans are not static. When a care team changes
> a patient's sodium limit, adjusts a caloric target, or flags a new allergen concern, the
> change is reflected in upcoming deliveries within 24 hours. Patients are notified of updates
> via the app and can review the reasoning behind any change before the next delivery is
> confirmed.

> Delivery logistics are handled entirely within the platform. Patients select a preferred
> delivery window during onboarding and can adjust their schedule at any time. Meals arrive
> chilled and labeled with heating instructions, nutritional information, and care-team notes
> where relevant. Every shipment includes a feedback card — digital or physical — that feeds
> directly into the patient's care record. Dietitians review meal feedback weekly and flag
> patterns that may indicate declining appetite, medication side effects, or shifting
> preferences.

> For care teams, the Cena Health dashboard provides a live view of each patient's meal
> compliance, delivery history, and logged feedback. Alerts surface automatically when a
> patient misses three or more consecutive meals, when a delivery is refused, or when feedback
> suggests a clinical concern. Care coordinators can message patients directly through the
> platform, approve plan updates submitted by dietitians, and export nutrition data for
> inclusion in clinical notes. The goal is not to replace the clinical encounter — it is to
> make every day between encounters count.
