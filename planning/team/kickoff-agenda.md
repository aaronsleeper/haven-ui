# Ava Kickoff — Meeting Agenda

*Duration: ~50 minutes. Attendees: Aaron, Andrey, Vanessa.*

---

## 1. The hypothesis (15 min)

Walk through the [Project Brief](project-brief.md) together.

**Key points to land:**
- The problem: administrative burden scales linearly with patients, breaks unit economics
- The bet: agents handle admin, humans provide judgment — same team serves 3x the patients
- The five principles — especially #1 (agents propose, humans dispose) since it governs how Andrey and Vanessa will interact with the system
- What Ava is not (not a chatbot, not an EHR, not replacing clinical judgment)

**Check for understanding:** Does this framing resonate? Does anyone see a flaw in the core hypothesis?

---

## 2. What we've built so far (10 min)

Walk through the [Status & Roadmap](status-and-roadmap.md).

**Key points to land:**
- 125+ planning docs — this is deeply thought through, not vaporware
- Show the structure: vision → architecture → workflows → expert system
- Show the admin app prototype (haven-ui) — make it tangible
- The expert system concept: AI domain experts that draft recommendations, accumulate knowledge, and improve through structured review cycles

**Check for understanding:** Is the planning depth surprising? Does the expert system concept make sense?

---

## 3. How you contribute (10 min)

Walk through the [Contribution Guide](contribution-guide.md).

**Key points to land:**
- The pattern: experts draft proposals, you review — you never start from a blank page
- Andrey: three architectural decisions pending. Each comes as a recommendation with tradeoffs. Your response can be "approved" or "approved with change: X"
- Vanessa: seven compliance/revenue cycle questions pending. Same pattern — proposals with context, not open-ended questions
- This is the "agents propose, humans dispose" principle applied to building Ava itself

**Check for understanding:** Does this model of contribution feel right? Is the ask clear?

---

## 4. Open discussion (15 min)

**Prompts if conversation stalls:**
- What's your biggest concern about this approach?
- What would make you more confident this will work?
- Is there anything we're not accounting for?
- Are there decisions you think should be made differently — or by different people?
