# Journey: Review Outcomes Report

## Journey Metadata
- **User:** Partner staff (e.g., Dr. Chen, UConn Health program director)
- **Goal:** Review program outcomes data for their referred patient population
- **Frequency:** Monthly or quarterly (per contract cadence)
- **Entry Point:** Email notification that a report is available, or portal login
- **Success Criteria:** Report reviewed, data understood, questions addressed
- **Duration:** 5-15 minutes

## Prerequisites
- Report has been generated by the agent and approved by coordinator for distribution
- Partner has portal access
- Sufficient patient data exists for meaningful reporting

---

### Step 3: Explore Detail (Optional)
- **Screen:** Partner Portal — report sections are expandable
- **User Action:** Clicks into a section for more detail:
  - Clinical outcomes → per-patient breakdown (if BAA permits identified data)
  - Meal program → satisfaction trends, recipe feedback
  - Engagement → patients at risk of disengagement
- **System Response:** Detail tables expand inline. Data is scoped to what the data sharing agreement permits.

### Step 4: Download or Share
- **Screen:** Partner Portal — report actions
- **User Action:** Downloads PDF for internal distribution or sharing with leadership
- **System Response:** PDF generated with Cena Health branding, date stamp, and confidentiality notice

## Alternative Paths

### Alt 1: De-identified Report (No BAA for Identified Data)
- **Trigger:** Partner's data sharing agreement limits to aggregate/de-identified data
- **Modified Steps:**
  - Report shows aggregate statistics only — no patient names or individual breakdowns
  - "Per-patient detail" sections are not available
  - Note at top: "This report contains aggregate data only per your data sharing agreement"
- **Outcome:** Partner gets population-level insights without PHI exposure

### Alt 2: Partner Has Questions About the Data
- **Trigger:** Partner doesn't understand a metric or wants context
- **Modified Steps:**
  - Each metric has an ℹ️ icon — tapping shows definition and methodology
  - "Contact your Cena Health coordinator" link at the bottom of every section
  - For complex questions: coordinator schedules a review call
- **Outcome:** Questions answered without ambiguity

### Alt 3: No Report Available Yet
- **Trigger:** Partner logs in but report hasn't been generated or approved yet
- **Modified Steps:**
  - Reports section shows: "Your next report is expected by April 15, 2026"
  - Previous reports remain accessible
- **Outcome:** Clear expectations, no empty state confusion

---

## Critical Moments

| Step | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | Partner overwhelmed by data volume | Summary metrics at top, detail expandable below |
| 2 | Partner misinterprets a metric | ℹ️ definitions available inline for every metric |
| 3 | Partner sees identified PHI they shouldn't | Data scoping enforced by agreement type — not a UI toggle |
| 4 | Report shared beyond authorized recipients | Confidentiality notice on PDF, access logged |

## Design Implications

1. **Lead with the headline numbers.** Partners — especially program directors and executives —
   want the top-line story first: how many patients, are outcomes improving, is the program
   working. Detail is secondary.

2. **Every metric has a definition.** "HbA1c improvement" could mean many things. Inline
   definitions prevent misinterpretation and build credibility.

3. **Data scoping is structural, not a setting.** Whether a partner sees identified or
   de-identified data is determined by their agreement type in the system — not a checkbox
   the partner or coordinator toggles. This is a compliance boundary.

4. **PDF export is essential.** Partners share reports internally. The PDF must be clean,
   branded, and self-contained — someone reading it without portal access should
   understand the data.

5. **Previous reports remain accessible.** Trend over time is the most compelling story.
   Partners should be able to compare Q1 to Q4 to Q3 without requesting old reports.
