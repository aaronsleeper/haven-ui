# Journey: Fulfill Daily Orders

## Journey Metadata
- **User:** Kitchen Staff (e.g., Carlos, lead cook at partnered kitchen)
- **Goal:** Review today's orders, prep meals, quality-check, and dispatch for delivery
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Entry Point:** Opens Kitchen App at start of shift
- **Success Criteria:** All orders prepped, quality-checked, and dispatched on time
- **Duration:** Full shift — the app is open throughout

## Prerequisites
- Orders have been generated by the agent from active meal prescriptions
- Recipe catalog is loaded with active recipes
- Grocery list was generated and procurement completed (day before)

### Step 2: Begin Prep — Update Order Status
- **Screen:** Left sidebar — tap individual order or use batch controls
- **User Action:** Marks orders as "prepping" — either individually or batch by recipe:
  - Tap a recipe group: "Start all Chicken & rice bowls (18)" → marks 18 orders as `prepping`
- **System Response:** Status counts update in real time. Left sidebar reflects current state.

### Step 3: Pack and Quality Check
- **Screen:** Center panel — order detail for packing
- **User Action:** For each completed order:
  1. Packs the meals
  2. Checks the packing slip against the order:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│   PACKING SLIP                  │
│   Order #2847                   │
│                                 │

│   □ Lentil soup                 │
│                                 │

│                                 │
│   Delivery: Mon Mar 27          │
│   Window: 10am – 2pm            │
│                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

  3. Taps **Quality checked** — confirms meals match the order, allergen flags verified
- **System Response:** Order status: `prepping` → `packed` → `quality_checked`

**PHI boundary reinforced:** Packing slip shows first name + last initial, delivery address,
meal contents, allergen flags, and delivery window. No diagnosis, insurance, clinical data,
or full last name.

### Step 4: Dispatch for Delivery
- **Screen:** Center panel — delivery dispatch view
- **User Action:** Assigns quality-checked orders to delivery:
  - Groups orders by delivery route (if multiple drivers)
  - Taps **Dispatch** for each batch
- **System Response:**
  - Order status: `quality_checked` → `dispatched`
  - Patient receives SMS: "Your Cena Health meals are on the way!"
  - Delivery tracking begins

### Step 5: Confirm Delivery
- **Screen:** Kitchen App — delivery tracking view (or driver's mobile interface)
- **User Action:** Driver confirms each delivery:
  - Tap **Delivered** at the door
  - If undeliverable: tap **Not delivered** with reason (not home, wrong address, access issue)
- **System Response:**
  - `dispatched` → `delivered` or `delivery_failed`
  - Failed deliveries: coordinator notified, patient contacted
  - Thread logs delivery confirmation with timestamp

### Step 6: End-of-Day Review
- **Screen:** Center panel — daily summary
- **User Action:** Reviews completion:

```
End of Day — Mar 27
──────────────────────────────────
Orders: 42
  ✅ Delivered: 40
  ⚠ Failed: 1 (not home — rescheduled)
  ⚠ Failed: 1 (wrong address — coordinator notified)

Quality checks: 42/42 complete
Food safety log: ✅ Temperature checks logged
──────────────────────────────────
```

- **Decision Point:** Any unresolved issues to flag?

## Alternative Paths

### Alt 1: Ingredient Shortfall
- **Trigger:** Grocery status shows a missing ingredient
- **Modified Steps:**
  - Morning summary flags: "⚠ Missing: frozen salmon — affects 7 orders"
  - Options: substitute recipe (agent suggests alternatives), delay affected orders, source ingredient
  - If substituting: agent checks that the substitute meets all dietary constraints for affected patients
  - Coordinator notified of any substitution that changes the patient's expected meal
- **Outcome:** Affected orders adjusted, patients notified if delivery content changes

### Alt 2: Recipe Ambiguity or Preparation Issue
- **Trigger:** Kitchen staff finds a recipe instruction unclear or impossible (wrong quantity, missing step)
- **Modified Steps:**
  - Staff taps **Flag issue** on the recipe
  - Types the issue: "Recipe says 200g chicken per serving but we received pre-cut 150g portions"
  - Issue logged in kitchen thread, coordinator notified
  - Kitchen staff makes a judgment call and documents what they actually did
- **Outcome:** Issue tracked, recipe updated for future orders

### Alt 3: Allergen Concern During Packing
- **Trigger:** Kitchen staff notices a potential cross-contamination issue or incorrect allergen flag
- **Modified Steps:**
  - Staff does NOT dispatch the order
  - Taps **Allergen concern** — order is held
  - Coordinator and RDN notified immediately
  - Order is either re-prepped or flagged for patient notification
- **Outcome:** Safety event handled before it reaches the patient

## Exception Handling

### Exception 1: Order Volume Exceeds Capacity
- **Cause:** More orders than the kitchen can fulfill in one day (new partner batch, surge)
- **Frequency:** Rare — should be caught during order generation
- **Severity:** Needs coordinator attention
- **Recovery:** Kitchen flags capacity issue, coordinator prioritizes orders or splits across delivery days
- **Prevention:** Agent checks kitchen capacity during order generation and warns before committing

### Exception 2: Delivery Driver Unavailable
- **Cause:** Driver no-show, vehicle issue
- **Frequency:** Occasional
- **Severity:** Affects all dispatched orders
- **Recovery:** Coordinator notified, backup driver arranged or deliveries rescheduled. Affected patients notified.
- **Prevention:** Delivery staffing managed outside the app — flagged as an ops risk

---

## Critical Moments

| Step | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Overwhelming order volume with no orientation | Daily summary gives the big picture before diving into individual orders |
| 3 | Allergen flag missed during packing | Allergen warnings are prominent on packing slips — red, large, impossible to miss |
| 3 | Wrong meal packed for a restricted patient | Quality check step is mandatory — cannot mark as dispatched without it |
| 5 | Delivery marked "delivered" but wasn't (driver error) | Patient feedback loop catches discrepancies |

## Design Implications

1. **Recipe-grouped production view.** Kitchen staff think in batches: "make 18 chicken bowls,"
   not "make one meal for each of 18 patients." The production summary groups by recipe,
   the individual orders are for packing and delivery.

2. **Allergen flags must be impossible to miss.** Red, bold, large text on every surface where
   the order appears — packing slip, order detail, daily summary. Allergen compliance is a
   safety-critical feature, not a data field.

3. **Status progression is tactile.** Pending → prepping → packed → quality_checked → dispatched
   → delivered. Each transition is a tap. The kitchen staff should feel the day progressing
   as statuses move forward.

4. **PHI is structurally absent.** Kitchen staff never see clinical data because it's not
   in the data model they access — not because of a permission toggle. The Kitchen App
   queries a different data scope than the Admin or Provider apps.

5. **The daily summary bookends the shift.** Start of day: what to make. End of day: what
   got delivered. Clean framing for a physical-work role that doesn't sit at a computer.
