Response scales: 31 items use 5 distinct response scales. This page demonstrates 2; see instrument-content-primary-sources.md § WHOQOL-HIV BREF for the full per-item mapping. The runner MUST bind each item's responseScale field — a single hardcoded scale is incorrect.

The 5 distinct scales (summary):

  • Not at all / A little / A moderate amount / Very much / An extreme amount — Q3–Q10 (8 items)
  • Not at all / A little / A moderate amount / Very much / Extremely — Q11–Q13 (3 items)
  • Not at all / A little / Moderately / Mostly / Completely — Q14–Q19 (6 items)
  • Very poor / Poor / Neither poor nor good / Good / Very good — Q1 global + Q20 (2 items)
  • Very dissatisfied / Dissatisfied / Neither satisfied nor dissatisfied / Satisfied / Very satisfied — Q2 global + Q21–Q30 (11 items)
  • Never / Seldom / Quite often / Very often / Always — Q31 (1 item; frequency scale; reverse-positive direction — flag for QA)

Scoring (computed at submit, never shown to patient): 6 domains — Physical (D1), Psychological (D2), Level of Independence (D3), Social Relations (D4), Environment (D5), Spiritual/Religion/Beliefs (D6) — plus 2 global items (Q1, Q2) not assigned to a scored domain. Raw domain scores can be transformed to 0–100 per the full WHOQOL-HIV scoring manual.

Reverse-scored items: 6 items use (6 − Qn) notation — Q3, Q4, Q5, Q8, Q9, Q10, Q31. See source artifact § Scoring for the full domain formulas.

Reference period: All items ask about the last two weeks — this phrase must be visible in the question header per instrument instructions.
