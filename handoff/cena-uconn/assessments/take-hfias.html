"No" skips directly to the next occurrence question. Q1 is rendered in full detail; Q2–Q9 follow the same shape (see footer).

Skip-logic shape — Q1 (same for Q2–Q9)

  • Yes → Q1a (frequency follow-up) appears → answer frequency → advance to Q2 (occurrence)
  • No → Q1a is skipped entirely → advance directly to Q2 (occurrence)
  • Score contribution: No = 0; Yes + Rarely = 1; Yes + Sometimes = 2; Yes + Often = 3
  • 9 occurrence gates × max 3 = HFIAS continuous score 0–27; scored at submit, not shown to patient

Q2–Q9 not rendered above in full

Q2 through Q9 follow the identical shape as Q1: a Yes/No occurrence question followed by a conditionally revealed three-option frequency follow-up (Rarely / Sometimes / Often). Q2 is rendered at reduced fidelity above to confirm the repeat shape. The eight remaining occurrence questions are listed here verbatim for completeness:

  • Q2. "…were you or any household member not able to eat the kinds of foods you preferred because of a lack of resources?"
  • Q3. "…did you or any household member have to eat a limited variety of foods due to a lack of resources?"
  • Q4. "…did you or any household member have to eat some foods that you really did not want to eat because of a lack of resources to obtain other types of food?"
  • Q5. "…did you or any household member have to eat a smaller meal than you felt you needed because there was not enough food?"
  • Q6. "…did you or any household member have to eat fewer meals in a day because there was not enough food?"
  • Q7. "…was there ever no food to eat of any kind in your household because of lack of resources to get food?"
  • Q8. "…did you or any household member go to sleep at night hungry because there was not enough food?"
  • Q9. "…did you or any household member go a whole day and night without eating anything because there was not enough food?"

All questions share the same response options (occurrence: Yes / No; frequency: Rarely / Sometimes / Often) and the same skip rule (No skips frequency; Yes reveals it).

HFIAP scoring — server-side only, never shown to patient

The HFIAS continuous score (0–27) sums the nine frequency responses (No = 0; Rarely = 1; Sometimes = 2; Often = 3). The HFIAS Access Prevalence (HFIAP) categorical score is derived from the continuous score:

  • Food Secure — Q1 = No; OR Q1 = Rarely AND Q2–Q9 all = No
  • Mildly Food Insecure — Q1 = Sometimes or Often; OR Q2 or Q3 = Yes (any frequency); AND Q4–Q9 all = No
  • Moderately Food Insecure — Q4 or Q5 or Q6 = Yes (any frequency); AND Q7–Q9 all = No
  • Severely Food Insecure — Q7 = Yes (any frequency); OR Q8 = Yes; OR Q9 = Yes

Categories are hierarchical — the household is classified at the most severe level that applies. Scoring runs at submit, routes to the clinician surface (cap-51), and is never surfaced to the patient.
