How many servings of fruit and vegetables per day do experts advise people to eat as a minimum? (One serving could be, for example, an apple or a handful of chopped carrots)

↑ GNKQ-R has 4 sections (Dietary Recommendations / Food Groups / Healthy Food Choices / Diet, Disease & Weight Management). Items vary in shape — single-select, food-list multi-item, agree/disagree. Sections can be administered independently per Kliemann 2016 scoring documentation.

Example B · Section 2: Food Groups · food-list multi-item (S2 Item 1)

This item type presents multiple foods as sub-questions with the same response scale. Each food is a separate scored point. Section 2 has 6 such food-list items (Items 1–6) totalling 26 sub-item scores, plus 4 single-select items (Items 7–10).

Source instrument: Kliemann, N., Wardle, J., Johnson, F., and Croker, H. (2016). "Reliability and validity of a revised version of the General Nutrition Knowledge Questionnaire." European Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 70, 1174–1180. doi:10.1038/ejcn.2016.87. Open-access; instrument text sourced from the open-access deposit at juanrevenga.com.

Sections and maxima: Section 1 (Dietary Recommendations) = 18 pts · Section 2 (Food Groups) = 36 pts · Section 3 (Healthy Food Choices) = 13 pts · Section 4 (Diet, Disease & Weight Management) = 21 pts · Total = 88 scored items. Section 5 (Demographics) is unscored.

Scoring: Correct-answer count per section. "Not sure" scores 0 (same as incorrect). Scoring is computed at submit. Per the no-score invariant (flow-take-assessment.md Step 5), scores are never shown to the patient — they route to the clinician surface (cap-51) only.
