HIPAA Authorization — summary

By joining the UConn Health nutrition program, you allow your care team to use and share certain health information — including your meals, dietary preferences, and check-in notes — to support your care.

Your information is shared only with the people on your care team and the kitchen partner who prepares your meals. It is protected under HIPAA and Connecticut state privacy law.

You can change your mind at any time by talking to your care coordinator. Leaving the program does not affect your other medical care or benefits.

Authorization to Use and Disclose Health Information

I authorize UConn Health and its food-as-medicine program partners ("Program") to use and disclose my protected health information as described below.

What information may be used: Dietary intake records, food preferences, self-reported health measurements (weight, blood pressure, A1C), program-related notes, and appointment history related to the nutrition program.

Who may receive this information: My assigned registered dietitian nutritionist, my care coordinator, the kitchen partner preparing my meals, and UConn Health program staff.

Purpose: To coordinate my nutrition care, prepare appropriate meals, and monitor my progress in the program.

Expiration: This authorization remains in effect until I withdraw it or leave the program.

Right to revoke: I may revoke this authorization in writing at any time. Revocation does not affect disclosures already made in good faith before my written revocation is received.

Consequences of not signing: I understand that refusing to sign this authorization means I cannot participate in the meal-delivery program. My other medical care at UConn Health is not affected.

A copy of this authorization is available on request from your care coordinator.
