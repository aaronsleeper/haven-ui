Renal-friendly meal composition

Dietary guideline · linked to chronic kidney disease diagnoses

Current Last updated Apr 14, 2026
Edit

Guideline text

Representative sample text — not sourced from Cena clinical records.

Prioritize meals that moderate dietary potassium, phosphorus, and sodium while maintaining adequate protein for the patient's stage of kidney function. Target sodium under 2,000 mg per day and limit high-phosphorus additives.

Favor lower-potassium produce and controlled portions of dairy. Coordinate with the patient's nephrology team before adjusting protein targets for patients approaching dialysis. Meal conformance scoring should flag any plan exceeding the sodium ceiling for review.
