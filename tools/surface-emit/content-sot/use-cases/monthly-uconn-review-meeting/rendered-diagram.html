Monthly UConn program review meeting — prep and facilitation

DRAFT • v0.1 • no SoP draft; deferred post-launch (catalog cap-48)

Cena's monthly UConn program review workflow runs on a monthly cadence: the reporting system fires a trigger when upstream monthly reports close; the agenda-generation pipeline produces a data-filled template; the meeting owner customizes talking points; the owner facilitates the review meeting with UConn participants; protocol refinement proposals route to Marrero (clinical) or Vanessa (operational), with a separate cross-cycle ownership transition from Vanessa to Marrero whose attestation gate is undefined.

Elements

  • Trigger — Monthly cadence + reports closed — system enquiry step in the SYSTEM lane; timing offset between report closure and meeting date, plus survey-integration semantics for Months 3/6/9, are unspecified.
  • Action — Auto-generate agenda template — agent action in the AGENT lane; the dashboard data-element set (Exhibit F) is pending Marinka and Andrey, so the agenda template's content is undefined.
  • Action — Customize talking points — human action in the HUMAN lane; customization criteria are undefined; no sight-check before facilitation.
  • Action — Facilitate monthly meeting — human action in the HUMAN lane with a dashed drop-line into the PARTNER lane encoding UConn participants attending; meeting format (in-person vs virtual) and UConn attendee list are unspecified; no facilitation-close attestation gate currently defined.
  • Hand-off (×2) — Protocol refinement routing + ownership transition arc — human hand-off in the HUMAN lane carrying both per-meeting refinement routing (clinical to Marrero, operational to Vanessa) AND the cross-cycle ownership transition from Vanessa to Marrero. Classification criteria are undefined; the ownership-transition attestation gate (a novel attestation shape where Vanessa attests that Marrero is ready to assume ownership) is undefined.

Relationships

  • Trigger → Auto-generate: reports-closed event fires the agenda pipeline (default arrow).
  • Auto-generate → Customize: agenda template hands off from agent to human meeting owner (default arrow).
  • Customize → Facilitate: customized agenda flows into the meeting (default arrow).
  • Facilitate → UConn participants: dashed drop-line encoding partner attendance (no separate fragment; participation, not handoff).
  • Facilitate → Routing: meeting notes carry refinement proposals into the routing fragment (emphasized arrow).

Five gap annotations are surfaced visibly on the diagram. Four are tier-gap (red-dotted): dashboard data-element set, customization criteria, meeting format and attendee list, and the ownership-transition gate. One is tier-assumption (sand-dashed): trigger timing and survey integration. The single critical annotation is the ownership-transition gate — a novel attestation shape (Vanessa attesting that Marrero is ready) with NO defined criterion, evidence requirement, or sign-off mechanism.
