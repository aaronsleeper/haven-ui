Written consent execution — Month 0 in-person

DRAFT • v0.1 • pending Marrero + Healthcare Data Governance review

Cena's in-person IRB written consent execution flow at the Month 0 visit. The Registered Dietitian executes consent with the participant, who signs on paper. The signed paper is handed to the Program Administrator the same day, who digitizes it and uploads the PDF to the participant's Athena record. Consent execution is the hard prerequisite gate before any baseline assessment.

Elements

  • Trigger — Month 0 visit, RD ready to execute — enquiry step in the HUMAN lane (Registered Dietitian); witness requirement TBD pending Marrero.
  • Attestation gate — Participant signs IRB paper form — patient attestation in the PATIENT lane; the only v0.1 use case where the attestor is the patient; decline-to-sign branch is a structural gap.
  • Hand-off — RD to Program Administrator (paper, same day) — cross-medium hand-off in the HUMAN lane; paper hand-delivered; chain-of-custody specifics pending Marrero.
  • Action — Digitize paper, upload PDF to Athena chart — action step in the SYSTEM lane (Program Administrator operates on Athena); PDF-landed verification is a structural gap.
  • Out-of-scope terminal — decline-or-reschedule (gap) — ghost terminal in the PATIENT lane reachable from the attestation gate if the participant declines; no documented downstream procedure.

Relationships

  • Trigger → Participant signs: visit confirmation routes to the attestation gate (emphasized arrow).
  • Participant signs → Hand-off: signed paper routes to the cross-medium hand-off (emphasized arrow).
  • Hand-off → Digitize and upload: received paper routes to the system-of-record action (emphasized arrow).
  • Participant signs → Out-of-scope decline-or-reschedule: declines routes to a ghost terminal with no documented procedure (muted dashed arrow).

Two structural gaps are surfaced visibly on the diagram: the decline-to-sign procedure (GAP — no documented re-consent, reschedule, or termination pathway) and PDF-landed verification (GAP — no explicit audit step confirms the PDF reached the participant's Athena record before downstream care-plan work proceeds). The use case has no AGENT lane (no agent-mediated steps), no PARTNER lane (fully internal to Cena + UConn site), and no watcher annotations (no documented supervisor role for the administrator's digitization step). The participant-as-attestor shape is unique among v0.1 use cases.
